# General Publisher Ad Copy & Creative Guidelines

1. All advertisements must comply with applicable law. Examples include TCPA, CAN-SPAM and state email statutes, Section 5 of the FTC Act, and the Telemarketing Sales Rule.
2. Advertisements should not contain materially false or misleading statements.
3. Advertisements should make it clear to the average consumer that, by clicking, they will be exploring ways to save on insurance or comparing insurance quotes.
4. No usage of the Progressive logo is allowed in any creative or copy marketing assets unless given specific written consent.
5. No mention of Covid-19, pandemic-related language, or imagery that could confuse consumers, such as doctors, lab coats, or stock market charts.
6. If the advertisement utilizes AI-generated images or video, a disclaimer must be displayed at the beginning or end of the ad.
7. No images of cash or checks may be used.
8. Do not incentivize consumers with discounts, free items, or experiences.
9. Do not use copy that paints the insurance industry or specific carriers in a negative light by creating a false sense of security, e.g. "Carriers don't want you to know this."
10. Do not use copy that calls attention or creates a negative perception of the role of agents/brokers. Specifically, do not use any assets regarding commissions paid to agents from consumer premium payments.
11. Do not use copy that provides a false sense of urgency for the consumer, e.g. discounts or pricing that has a "limited time" or "will run".
12. Do not state or imply that an insurance carrier or comparison site has done something it has not, such as refreshed their rates, re-checked their application, or used their preferences to get them new matches.
13. Do not use copy broadly claiming new pricing for certain states or consumer segments.
14. Do not target seniors or other vulnerable populations.
15. Do not state or imply that the consumer qualifies for some program or savings.
16. Do not imply association with any government program. No images/indication of government insignia, buildings, benefits, or figures.
17. Do not state or imply something about the consumer's driving record or claims history.
18. No use of insurance company brand names or logos.
19. Do not use another brand's intellectual property, such as Costco or WalMart.
20. No refund or rebate language that could mislead or confuse consumers.
21. Acceptable creative copy may include phrases such as: "Save up to," "As much as," "As low as," "Could save," or "See if you qualify."
22. Any savings numbers or percentages must be properly sourced, clearly referenced in a disclaimer, and not hidden from consumers.

## Category-Specific Creative Restrictions

### Auto

- No imagery of car wrecks, car crashes, or any likeness to fearmongering.
- No language referencing DUIs hidden from consumers.

### Home/Renters

- No imagery of house fires, burnings, or any likeness to fearmongering.

### Health

- No imagery of dying, severe illness, or any likeness to fearmongering.
